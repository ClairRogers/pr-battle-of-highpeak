Paradise Rising: Trial 004
The Battle of Highpeak
The Story
  • Entry 01

The darkness that followed the wave was not unwelcome. It was heavy, numbing, so dark that, while you’re stuck in it, you almost completely forget about everything that is going on around you. You forget your power, the games, the death. Yourself.

But it doesn’t last. The moment you are suddenly aware that you feel like you’re drowning, is the moment that you begin to kick and thrash, and in the next moment the water pulls away, leaving you coughing and heaving on a cool, smooth surface.

When you finally look up, you’re in a different world.

You’re laying amongst your teammates on a beautiful terrace; high pillars of marble soar over your head, vibrant green vines wrapping around them, holding an arching ceiling over your head with hundreds of colors painted into a beautiful, ornate mural. Warm sunlight hits your pelt from the open sides, and to the south there is a staircase that leads off into a huge, sprawling lake. Under the water you can see more marble buildings, most almost completely broken, but some are nearly entirely intact. The water is crystal clear, all the way to the bottom, and you can see schools of fish swimming lazily through the ruins. Snow-capped mountains cut into the brilliant blue sky in the distance.

It would all be so peaceful and calm if it weren’t for the teammates who don’t stand up. The reminders of what you had just seen. The exhaustion hangs on your bones. You feel so, so tired.

Syaoran and Kismet lay dead on the smooth marble. And when you look again, two more cats don’t get back up; Osmani and Ivah are stiff, their bodies already growing cold. Someone tries to revive them, desperately, but it’s too late. They had drowned in the tidal wave.

“Well," you hear a voice rise above the rest. You look up, and perched above you on a high ledge is Oordeel; it feels strange to see him with his wings, with his tail vanishing into a plume of smoke, his eyes a bit too vibrant, his neck adorned in the high collar. He’s staring at the two drowned cats. “Guess I didn’t pull us here fast enough. That was an unfortunate development, Helena getting her power back. Should have seen it coming." He looks back up at you, his expression serious and cold. “It just means things are about to start moving much more quickly. Now that Helena has her wings back, her next move will be on the Legendaries; she will want to get them out of her way."

“We need to save them."

He stands, stretching his wings, and you think that he’s actually grown since getting his power back. “You can’t take on Helena on your own; and neither can I, not with Kaulu giving her the power she needs. You’re too inexperienced, and I don’t know what Helena’s plans are. I’m going to go figure it out. I don’t know how long I’ll be gone. But while I’m figuring out what our next move will be, you will be safe here. I created this place out of Helena’s sight; you’re hidden. She won’t come looking for you. Hide, stay here, and practice your damn powers. I assume some of you have discovered that you can align, yes? Figure that out."

He stretches his wings and turns to take off, but then glances at you one more time. “Be ready when I return. A war has begun. And if you’re not prepared, you will all die."

With a rush of wind, Oordeel’s wings push him into the sky, and he’s gone.
